The First Guru of Sikhs - "Shri Guru Nanak Dev Ji"

The First Guru of Sikhs

Shri Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder and the first Guru of Sikhs, was born in year 1469 to Mehta Kalu ji(father) and Mata Tripta ji(mother), in the village Talwandi Sabo, which is now known as Nankiana Sahib in Lahore, Pakistan. He also had an elder sister named Bibi Nanki ji. Guru Nanak Dev ji was an extra ordinary child, blessed with a deeply contemplative mind and rational thinking. He had mastered various religious texts and languages such as Sanskriti, Hindi and Persian.

Guru Nanak Dev ji was married to Mata Sulakhni ji in year 1487, and had two sons, Shri Chand and Lakhmi Das. Guru Nanak Dev ji's childhood friend Bhai Mardana ji accompanied the family to the town of Sultanpur Lodhi and later on his spiritual journeys.

Teachings of Shri Guru Nanak Dev Ji

Guru Nanak Dev ji founded three pillars of Sikhism.

  1. Naam Japo: Guru Nanak Dev ji asked Sikhs to practise Simran and Naam Japo - which refers to the meditation of God through reciting, chanting, singing, and constant rememberence of God.
  2. Kirat Karo: Guru Nanak ji expected Sikhs to practise Kirat Karni - referring to earn livelihood honestly by devoting both physical and mental efforts to accept pains and gains as God's blessings.
  3. Vand Chako: Guru Nanak Dev ji asked Sikhs to to share their wealth while practising Vand Chako (Share and consume together) within the society.

Four Great Spiritual Journeys

Shri Guru Nanak Dev ji led four great spiritual journeys by traveling to all parts of India, Sri Lanka, Arabia and Persia. He visited various pilgrimage sites such as temples, mosques and many more, where he spoke out against empty religious rituals, caste system, sacrifice of widows, and pilgrimages to learn true religion.
